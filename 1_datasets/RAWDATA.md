# Raw Datasets for Access to Higher Education for Refugee Youth Research

The folder `raw_datasets_files` contains the raw sources used for our research into how refugee youth access higher education and which support pathways (scholarships, NGO programmes, online learning) are available to them.  
As per the repository guidelines, these files are kept in their original, unmodified state.

---

## 1. UNHCR – Higher Education and Skills (Global Overview)

**Source:** United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR)  

**Official Source Link:**  
https://www.unhcr.org/what-we-do/build-better-futures/education/higher-education-and-skills  

**Description:**  
Global overview of refugee access to higher education, summarising key barriers such as financial constraints, documentation gaps, language issues and limited digital access.  

**Relevance to Problem:**  
Provides baseline context and headline figures on how many refugees reach tertiary education, which we use to frame the global scale of the issue.

**Classification by Data Type:**

- **Qualitative (Descriptive) Data:** Narrative descriptions of barriers, pathways and policies.  
- **Quantitative (Numerical) Data:** High-level figures such as global refugee tertiary enrolment share.

**Classification by Structure:**

- **Semi-structured Data:** Web text and PDF content organised in sections but not strictly tabular.

**Classification by Access Type:**

- **Public Data:** Freely available from the UNHCR website.

## 2. UNHCR – 15by30 Higher-Education Roadmap

**Source:** UNHCR  

**Official Source Link:**  
https://www.unhcr.org/sites/default/files/2023-12/15by30-roadmap.pdf  

**Description:**  
Strategy document setting the goal of increasing global refugee higher-education enrolment to 15% by 2030 and outlining five main pathways (national systems, scholarships, connected learning, TVET, self-reliance).  

**Relevance to Problem:**  
Used to understand international targets and recommended interventions that can inspire or benchmark our proposed solutions.

**Classification by Data Type:**

- **Qualitative (Descriptive) Data:** Strategic priorities, pathways, policy arguments.  
- **Quantitative (Numerical) Data:** Target indicators (7% → 15% enrolment, expansion goals).

**Classification by Structure:**

- **Structured Document:** PDF report with defined sections, figures and tables.

**Classification by Access Type:**

- **Public Data:** Downloadable from the UNHCR website.

## 3. DAFI Tertiary Scholarship Programme

**Source:** UNHCR & German Federal Foreign Office  

**Official Source Link:**  
https://www.unhcr.org/what-we-do/build-better-futures/education/higher-education-and-skills/dafi-tertiary-scholarship-0  

**Description:**  
One of the largest global scholarship schemes for refugees, providing tuition support, stipends and mentoring for undergraduate study in host countries.  

**Relevance to Problem:**  
Serves as a concrete example of how scholarships expand higher-education access and what kinds of support structures are effective.

**Classification by Data Type:**

- **Qualitative (Descriptive) Data:** Programme design, eligibility criteria, types of support.  
- **Quantitative (Numerical) Data:** Numbers of scholars, participating countries (where reported).

**Classification by Structure:**

- **Semi-structured Data:** Web pages and brochures with sections and summary tables.

**Classification by Access Type:**

- **Public Data:** Programme information is freely available online.

## 4. IOM – Higher-Education Scholarships for Refugees (Feasibility Study)

**Source:** International Organization for Migration (IOM)  

**Official Source Link:**  
https://www.iom.int/sites/g/files/tmzbdl486/files/documents/ERN%2Bfeasibility-study-Higher-Education-Scholarships-for-Refugees.pdf  

**Description:**  
Analytical study comparing different scholarship models (in-country, third-country, online) and identifying good practices, challenges and design considerations.  

**Relevance to Problem:**  
Provides evidence and design lessons for scholarship-based interventions aimed at refugee and displaced youth.

**Classification by Data Type:**

- **Qualitative (Descriptive) Data:** Case studies, interviews, comparative analysis.  
- **Quantitative (Numerical) Data:** Summary figures on numbers of schemes or participants where available.

**Classification by Structure:**

- **Structured Data:** PDF report with chapters, tables and annexes.

**Classification by Access Type:**

- **Public Data:** Free PDF published on the IOM website.

## 5. El-Ghali et al. (2019) – Tertiary Education for Syrian Refugees in Lebanon

**Source:** Issam Fares Institute for Public Policy and International Affairs, American University of Beirut  

**Official Source Link:**  
https://www.aub.edu.lb/ifi/Documents/publications/policy_briefs/2018-2019/20190402_tertiary_education_syrian_refugees_lebanon.pdf  

**Description:**  
Policy brief describing financial, legal and language barriers facing Syrian refugees who try to enter Lebanese universities, with basic enrolment statistics from 2013–2018.  

**Relevance to Problem:**  
Used as a country case study to show how global challenges appear in a specific context and to identify gaps in existing support programmes.

**Classification by Data Type:**

- **Quantitative (Numerical) Data:** Enrolment counts and simple percentages over time.  
- **Qualitative (Descriptive) Data:** Explanations of barriers, policy environment and support gaps.

**Classification by Structure:**

- **Structured Data:** Short PDF with clear sections and a small number of tables/figures.

**Classification by Access Type:**

- **Public Data:** Openly accessible policy brief.

## 6. El-Ghali (2019) – Syrian Refugee Students in Lebanese Higher Education

**Source:** Issam Fares Institute, American University of Beirut  

**Official Source Link:**  
https://www.aub.edu.lb/ifi/Documents/publications/research_reports/2019-2020/20190107_syrian_refugee_students_in_lebanese_higher_education.pdf  

**Description:**  
Research report focusing on documentation and recognition-of-qualifications issues for Syrian refugee students, including tables and charts on enrolment, exam outcomes and diploma equivalence.  

**Relevance to Problem:**  
Provides detailed quantitative and qualitative evidence on legal and administrative barriers that prevent refugee youth from accessing or completing higher education.

**Classification by Data Type:**

- **Quantitative (Numerical) Data:** Tables with enrolment, exam and recognition statistics.  
- **Qualitative (Descriptive) Data:** Interview findings, policy discussion and recommendations.

**Classification by Structure:**

- **Structured Data:** Full research report with chapters, tables, charts and annexes.

**Classification by Access Type:**

- **Public Data:** Report is freely downloadable from the AUB / IFI website.
